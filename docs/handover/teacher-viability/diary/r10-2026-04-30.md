## Step 6 — Teacher delta + per-task pattern

**Aggregate (gemini-pro − gemma-react step-coverage): +0.166** (0.473 vs 0.307). **Gate ≥ +0.10 cleared.**

**Win/tie/loss (step-cov)**: 8 wins / 11 ties / 1 loss (where loss = `hard-volvo-ex90`, only -0.17 below regression flag).

**Pattern**: Pro wins concentrate on `journey-*` tasks (medium-hard agentic browsing — the bridge tasks where distillation has signal). Ties cluster on calibrations (already at ceiling) and trivials (already passing). Loss is a single exotic configurator, below the regression-flag threshold. This is the right shape for a teacher.

**Caveats** (flagged in report and below):

1. **Schema-invalid count drift on gemma lane**: R10 5/20 vs R9 2/20. If genuine regression, gemma baseline is artificially low and the teacher delta is closer to +0.10 than +0.17. Pro 3 reaches more KeyNodes but commits "done" before satisfying final-state. Mean finalState 0.100 (Pro) < 0.150 (gemma). Distillation data export will need a filter for premature-completion traces, otherwise the LoRA inherits this behavior.

3. **Cost**: gemini-react lane consumed ~4.54M total executor tokens. Conservative estimate ~$17–25 (within plan's $30–60 budget).

## Reviewer T2 variance check

Reviewer ran a third zero-code-change gemma-react lane (`evals/traces/wave-r10-gemma-revisit/`) to characterize the schema-invalid count distribution.

| Run | Schema-invalid count | Affected tasks |
|---|---|---|
| R9 baseline (`wave-r9-bridge-coerce`) | 2/20 | `hard-volvo-ex90-configurator`, `journey-9-form-wizard` |
| R10 T1 sweep (`wave-r10-pro-preview`) | 5/20 | `hard-volvo-ex90-configurator`, `journey-5-insurance-quote`, `journey-6-media-streaming`, `journey-10-marketplace-filter`, `trivial-1-example-homepage` |

**Observations**:
- Distribution: {2, 4, 5}/20 across three zero-code-change runs. **Task-stochastic noise, not regression.**
- Affected task sets are largely disjoint across runs (`hard-volvo` overlaps R9↔T1; `trivial-1` overlaps T1↔revisit). No task fails schema-invalid in all three.
- R8 invariant (empty-content) holds 0/20 across all three runs.

**Reviewer's gate-recheck math** (worst-case bounds the teacher delta against the highest gemma run):
- Best-case gemma baseline (lowest schema-invalid count): R9 step-cov 0.372 → teacher delta = 0.473 − 0.372 = **+0.101** (gate cleared by 0.001).
- Worst-case gemma baseline (T1 sweep depressed by 5/20): T1 step-cov 0.307 → teacher delta = 0.473 − 0.307 = **+0.166** (gate cleared by 0.066).

Even with the most pessimistic assumption — that the gemma 0.372 of R9 is the "true" mean and the 0.307 of T1 is just noise — the teacher delta clears the +0.10 gate.

**Reviewer verdict**: APPROVED. Per lead, the 8 Pro wins are all genuine; the two `gemma-better` deltas the report flagged are confounds (schema-invalid aborts on tasks where Pro 3 reached more KeyNodes). Pro 3 is the teacher.

**Lessons for future wave plans** (carried forward as closure notes in the Path A section below):
- The R9 "≤2/20 schema-invalid" gate was anchored on a single sample; the actual distribution is 2-5/20. Future wave plans should widen to "≤5/20" or treat as a distribution, not a point gate.
- Distillation training-data export must filter on `RUN_COMPLETED:passed AND finalState == 1.0 AND step-coverage == 1.0` — Pro 3's stopping-criterion has two failure shapes (premature + over-execution) that pollute LoRA training.
- Pro 3 SKU resolution: `gemini-3-pro-preview` → server-side `gemini-3.1-pro-preview` per `modelVersion`. Emits `thoughtSignature` + ~140 reasoning tokens per round. Note for future preview-SKU rotations.
