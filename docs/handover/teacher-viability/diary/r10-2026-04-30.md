### Closure notes for the distillation wave

Three items reviewer + lead both flagged for the next wave (browsing-gemma LoRA training) to inherit:

1. **Distillation training-data export filter** must be `RUN_COMPLETED:passed AND finalState == 1.0 AND step-coverage == 1.0`, **NOT** just `passed`. Pro 3's stopping-criterion has two distinct failure shapes that both pollute LoRA training:
   - **Premature** — Pro declares done before satisfying the final-state assertion (13/20 RUN_COMPLETED:failed on the gemini-react lane in T1). Exemplar: most journey-* tasks where Pro reached fewer KeyNodes than gemma.
   - **Over-execution** — Pro hits all expected STEP_DONEs then keeps tooling past success, often to MAX_TOOL_ROUNDS. Exemplars: `calibration-2-single-nav-news` (Pro hit all 3 STEP_DONEs then ran to MAX_TOOL_ROUNDS), `moderate-2-mdn-web-api-detail` (Pro went 2 nav steps deeper than the target).

   A status-only filter passes the over-execution case (RUN_COMPLETED:passed) and the premature case fails outright; only the conjunction with finalState=1.0 AND step-coverage=1.0 isolates the trajectories that are actually clean teacher data.

2. **R9-style point gates should be widened to distributions.** The R9 "≤2/20 schema-invalid" gate was anchored on a single sample; three-run variance shows the actual band is 2-5/20. Future wave plans should widen to "≤5/20" or treat schema-invalid count as a distribution, not a hard point threshold. Worth a one-line callout in `project_baseline_eval_strategy.md` so future waves don't anchor on single-sample gate values.

3. **Pro 3 SKU resolution detail.** `gemini-3-pro-preview` resolves server-side to `gemini-3.1-pro-preview` per the `modelVersion` field of the `generateContent` response. Pro 3 emits `thoughtSignature` + ~140 reasoning tokens per round transparently behind the public API (billed as output tokens). Capture for future preview-SKU rotations — Google's preview SKU names rotate periodically, and the env-var override path at `gemini.ts:70` is the primary diagnostic.
